In this project I set out to create a customisable Arduino based automotive gauge - ArduGauge. The hardware is designed around an Atmega328P microcontroller, the same chip found in Arduino UNO's & Nano's. This allowed me to develop a gauge which is easily programmable whilst maintaining a relatively low cost. The enlcousure is sized to be a direct replacemnt for a standard 52mm automotive gauge.

Key features:

  • 1.28" LCD
  • 4 analog inputs
  • SPI header
  • I2C header
  • Onboard voltage regulator and filtering

Develolment started with an Arduino Uno and a round LCD to become familiarised with the hardware and its limitations. The limiting factor is the rate at which the Arduino can refresh the display. Along side the electronic development, a housing design was developed to fit into a 52mm gauge holder and employs the same threaded collar retainer as found on more modern VDO gauges.

Due to the range of I/O available, and paired with an Arduino CAN-bus adapter, ArduGauge could be used to display just about any data you could think of. Below are some of the use cases i have tested:

Temperature Gauge

As seen in the development section, the most simple of readouts is with the use of an analog sensor, such as a thermisotr. The push button is used to cycle between a digital or analog guage style.

Accelerometer

Connected to an MPU6050 accelerometer/gyroscope via the I2C bus, the ArduGauge can display the longtidudonal and lateral acceleration of the vehicle in which it is mounted.

Clock

A simple clock face.
